## üìù Creating Your First Invoice

### What is an Invoice?
**Simple explanation:** A bill that shows what was sold, to whom, and for how much money.

### Types of Invoices:

1. **Sales Invoice** üì§
   - When YOU sell something to a customer
   - Money comes TO you
   - Example: You sold groceries to Mr. Sharma

2. **Purchase Invoice** üì•
   - When YOU buy something from a supplier
   - Money goes FROM you
   - Example: You bought vegetables from a wholesaler

---

### Creating a Sales Invoice (Selling Something)

**Step-by-Step Guide:**

#### Step 1: Start Creating
1. Tap the **"Create Invoice +"** button (bottom right)
2. Select **"Sales Invoice"** from the popup

#### Step 2: Add Customer Details
You'll see a customer input box:

**Option A - New Customer:**
1. Type the customer's name (e.g., "Ramesh Kumar")
2. Enter phone number (optional but recommended)
3. Tap outside the box to save

**Option B - Existing Customer:**
1. Start typing the name
2. Select from the dropdown list that appears

**Why add phone number?**
- Send WhatsApp reminders for payments
- Easy to contact for follow-ups

#### Step 3: Choose Items
You'll see a list of products:

1. **Search for items:** Use the search box at top
2. **Filter by category:** Tap category chips (Kitchen, Cleaning, etc.)
3. **Select items:** Tap on any item you're selling

For each item:
- **Quantity:** Tap the number between + and - buttons to type directly
  - Or use + and - buttons to increase/decrease
  - Example: Selling 5 packets of rice? Type "5"

- **Price shown:** Current rate per item (you can update this in Items screen)
- **Stock alert:** Shows available stock for sales invoices

**Example:**
```
Item: Basmati Rice (5kg)
Price: ‚Çπ450 per bag
Quantity: 3 bags
Total: ‚Çπ1,350
```

#### Step 4: Review & Payment
After adding all items:

1. **Check the summary:**
   - Items list with quantities
   - Subtotal (items total)
   - Total Amount (final bill)

2. **Payment Details:**
   - **Amount Paid:** How much customer paid NOW
     - Example: Total is ‚Çπ1,000 but customer paid ‚Çπ600
   - **Balance:** Automatically calculated
     - Remaining: ‚Çπ400 (customer will pay later)

3. **Payment Mode:** Select how they paid
   - Cash üíµ
   - UPI/Digital üì±
   - Card üí≥
   - Credit (will pay later) ‚è≥

#### Step 5: Save Invoice
1. Tap **"Save Invoice"** button
2. Success message appears ‚úÖ
3. Invoice is saved and visible in Invoices list

### Creating a Purchase Invoice (Buying Something)

**When to use:** When you buy stock/supplies from a wholesaler or supplier.

**Steps are similar to Sales Invoice, but:**
- No stock checks (you're adding to inventory, not reducing)

**Example Scenario:**

## üì¶ Tracking Inventory

### What is Inventory?
**Simple:** The list of products you have in stock (available to sell).

### Viewing Your Inventory

1. Open the **side menu** (‚ò∞ icon, top-left of Home screen)
2. Tap **"Inventory"**

You'll see:
- List of all your products
- Current stock quantity
- Stock value (quantity √ó price)
- Low stock alerts (‚ö†Ô∏è red indicator)

### Understanding Stock Levels

**Color Codes:**
- ‚úÖ **Green:** Good stock (plenty available)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Orange:** Low stock (need to reorder soon)
- ‚ùå **Red:** Out of stock (cannot sell)

### How Stock Updates Automatically

**Automatic Updates:**
- **Sales Invoice:** Stock decreases (you sold items)
- **Purchase Invoice:** Stock increases (you bought items)
- **Returns:** Stock adjusts accordingly

**Example:**
```
Initial Stock: Rice bags = 50
After Sales Invoice (sold 5 bags): Stock = 45
After Purchase Invoice (bought 20 bags): Stock = 65
```

### Managing Item Prices

1. Open side menu (‚ò∞)
2. Tap **"Manage Item Rates"**
3. See list of all products with current prices
4. Tap any item to update its price
5. Enter new price and save

**Note:** Price changes apply to future invoices only (old invoices remain unchanged).

## üîÑ Handling Returns

### What are Returns?

**Sales Return:** Customer returned something they bought
**Purchase Return:** You returned something to your supplier

### Processing a Return

#### Step 1: Navigate to Returns
1. Open side menu (‚ò∞)
2. Tap **"Returns"**
3. Choose tab:
   - **Sales Returns:** Customers returning items to you
   - **Purchase Returns:** You returning items to suppliers

#### Step 2: Create a New Return

1. Find the original invoice
2. Select items being returned
3. Choose return reason:
   - Damaged product
   - Wrong item delivered
   - Quality issue
   - Customer changed mind
   - Other

4. Confirm return amount

#### Step 3: Return Processing

**For Sales Returns:**
- Return amount is saved to customer's account
- Next time they buy, amount is automatically adjusted
- Stock increases (items back in inventory)

**For Purchase Returns:**
- Return amount is recorded
- Stock decreases (items removed)

**Example:**
```
Original Sale: ‚Çπ1,000 to Mr. Sharma
Return: 1 item worth ‚Çπ200
Credit Balance: ‚Çπ200 for Mr. Sharma

Next Purchase by Mr. Sharma:
New Bill: ‚Çπ800
Credit Applied: -‚Çπ200
Amount to Pay: ‚Çπ600
```

## üìä Understanding Analytics

### What are Analytics?
**Simple:** Reports that show how your business is doing - like a report card for your shop!

### Viewing Analytics

1. Tap **"Analytics"** icon (üìä) in bottom navigation
2. See visual charts and reports

### Key Metrics Explained

#### 1. **Revenue Trends**
- **What it shows:** Money earned over time
- **How to read:**
  - Upward line = Business growing ‚úÖ
  - Downward line = Sales decreasing ‚ö†Ô∏è
  - Flat line = Stable sales

#### 2. **Top Selling Items**
- **What it shows:** Which products sell the most
- **Why useful:** Know what to stock more of
- **Example:** If "Rice" is #1, keep plenty in stock!

#### 3. **Customer Analytics**
- **Top Customers:** Who buys the most
- **Customer Dues:** Who owes you money
- **Why useful:** Focus on valuable customers

#### 4. **Profit Margins**
- **What it shows:** How much profit each sale makes
- **Formula:** Selling Price - Buying Price = Profit
- **Example:**
  ```
  Bought rice @ ‚Çπ400/bag
  Sold rice @ ‚Çπ500/bag
  Profit = ‚Çπ100 per bag
  ```

### Date Range Filtering

## üîß Troubleshooting

### Problem: App is Slow

**Solutions:**
1. Close and reopen the app
2. Check internet connection
3. Clear app cache (in phone settings)
4. Ensure phone has sufficient storage

### Problem: Invoice Not Saving

**Check:**
- ‚úÖ All required fields filled
- ‚úÖ Customer name entered
- ‚úÖ At least one item selected
- ‚úÖ Internet connection active

**Fix:** Review error message and fill missing information.

### Problem: Can't Find a Customer

**Reasons:**
- Name spelled differently
- Customer not added yet
- Search term too specific

**Solutions:**
- Try typing just first name
- Check spelling
- Add as new customer if needed

### Problem: Stock Shows Wrong Number

**Possible causes:**
- Recent invoice not synced yet
- Manual adjustment needed
- Return not processed

**Fix:**
1. Refresh the inventory screen (pull down)
2. Check recent invoices
3. Contact support if issue persists

### Problem: Login Issues

**Solutions:**
1. Verify email and password
2. Use **"Forgot Password"** if needed
3. Check internet connection
4. Ensure app is updated to latest version

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q1: Is my data safe?**
**A:** Yes! All data is encrypted and stored securely in the cloud. Only you can access it.

**Q2: Can I use InvoiceFlow offline?**
**A:** Yes! Create invoices offline. Data syncs automatically when internet returns.

**Q3: How many invoices can I create?**
**A:** Unlimited! No restrictions on invoices, customers, or products.

**Q4: Can multiple people use the same account?**
**A:** Currently, one account per user. Each user needs their own login.

**Q5: Is there a monthly fee?**
**A:** Check the app or website for current pricing plans.

---

### Invoice Questions

**Q6: Can I edit an invoice after saving?**
**A:** No, invoices are final once saved (for accounting accuracy). Create a return or new invoice instead.

**Q7: Can I delete an invoice?**
**A:** Invoices can be cancelled but not deleted (for record-keeping). Cancelled invoices show in reports.

**Q8: How do I print invoices?**
**A:**
1. Open invoice details
2. Tap **Share** icon
3. Select **Print** or **Save as PDF**

**Q9: Can I customize invoice format?**
**A:** Currently uses standard format. Customization may be available in future updates.

**Q10: What if I made a mistake in an invoice?**
**A:** Create a return for the incorrect item and create a new invoice with correct details.

---

### Customer Questions

**Q11: Can I add customer address?**
**A:** Currently supports name and phone. Address field may be added in future versions.

**Q12: How do I send invoice to customer?**
**A:**
1. Open invoice
2. Tap **Share** button
3. Choose WhatsApp, Email, or SMS

**Q13: Can I block a customer?**
**A:** No blocking feature. Simply stop creating invoices for that customer.

---

### Inventory Questions

**Q14: How do I add new products?**
**A:** Products are added automatically when used in an invoice. Or use "Manage Item Rates" to add manually.

**Q15: Can I set low stock alerts?**
**A:** Yes! App automatically alerts when stock falls below reorder level.

**Q16: What if I have the same product in different sizes?**
**A:** Add each size as a separate item:
- Rice 1kg
- Rice 5kg
- Rice 10kg

**Q17: Can I adjust stock manually?**
**A:** Yes, use Purchase Invoice with "Stock Adjustment" as reason.

---

### Returns Questions

**Q18: Does return affect inventory?**
**A:** Yes!
- Sales return: Stock increases
- Purchase return: Stock decreases

**Q19: How long can I process a return?**
**A:** No time limit. Process returns anytime.

**Q20: Can I do partial returns?**
**A:** Yes! Select only the items/quantities being returned.

---

### Analytics Questions

**Q21: How often do reports update?**
**A:** Real-time! Reports reflect latest data instantly.

**Q22: Can I see last year's data?**
**A:** Yes, use custom date range to select any period.

**Q23: What does "Net Profit" mean?**
**A:** Revenue (sales) minus Expenses (purchases) = Your actual earnings.

## üìù Glossary

**Simple definitions of common terms:**

- **Invoice:** A bill showing what was sold and for how much
- **Revenue:** Total money earned from sales
- **Expense:** Total money spent on purchases
- **Profit:** Revenue minus Expenses
- **Stock/Inventory:** Products available to sell
- **Balance Due:** Money still owed (not yet paid)
- **Credit:** Paying later instead of immediately
- **Return:** Giving back purchased items
- **Analytics:** Reports showing business performance
- **Sync:** Updating data across devices
- **Offline:** Working without internet connection
